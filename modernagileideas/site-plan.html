Content

Home page

This site is to help anyone interested in modern agile to understand some of the basics.
The Modern Agile badge encapsulates the four principles of Modern Agile.
The horizontal elements from the badge are Experiment and Learn Rapidly & Deliver Value Continuously.
  • The horizontal elements primarily represent the mechanics of the modern agile process.
  • Experiment and Learn Rapidly speaks to the requirement to try new things and always improve.
  • Deliver value Continuously speaks to the requirement to actually get work done and deliver something that adds value.
The Vertical elements from the badge are Make People Awesome and Make Safety a Prerequisite.
  • The vertical elements primarily represent the human factor of the modern agile process.
  • Make People Awesome refers to both those who are working on the code as well as our customers.
  • The goal is always to make people Awesome or at least more awesome than they were.
Make Safety a Prerequisite is the element that recognizes we need to feel safe if we are going to do our best work.
Each of the following pages will contain some ideal ways to implement each of these dimensions and values.
Each page will also talk about how the Transformers Automation Team at AdvancedMD is currently implementing these principles.

Horizontal Experiment And Learn Rapidly & Deliver Value Continuously

The horizontal aspect of the Modern agile badge represents primarily the mechanics and the product we are trying to produce.
The Although all the values work together the horizontal values are designed to focus on the how of building the product.
Experiment and Learn Rapidly is the first of the horizontal values.
Experimentation is an essential part of agile software development.
Experimentation provides the following advantages.
  • The ability to try things to make customers awesome.
  • The ability to build software with your desired quality attributes.
Learn Rapidly is the second part it provides.
  • Emphasizes that Experimentation is designed to be iterative in short but meaningful cycles
  • Experimentation without learning is not adding value even if you are learning what not to do learning is an essential part of the process.
Deliver Value Continuously is the second of the horizontal values.
Delivery is the first element.
  • To add value you have to deliver.
  • You don't have to deliver everything just something.
  • What you deliver should work.
Value is what we are intended to deliver.
  • Value means you give something that people find useful.
  • It again isn't necessarily all the value but enough value that people could see how they would use it.
Continuously is how we deliver.
  • At every opportunity we should deliver.
  • This implies Continuous Delivery and Continuous Integration (CI/CD)
  • Delivering in a just in time manner puts value in front of people and allows us to begin to adjust.

Vertical Make People Awesome & Make Safety a Prerequisite

The vertical aspect of the Modern Agile badge represents primarily the human element of the product we are trying to produce.
The Although all the values work together the vertical values are designed to focus on the humanity of building a product.
Make People Awesome is the first of the vertical values.
This is a broad value that requires you to think who am I trying to make awesome and what can I do to make them awesome.
The people to be made awesome are everyone involved in the software including customers, stakeholders, and the team.
  • Awesomeness means different things to different people in the process.
  • Customers are made awesome whenever you provide the ability to do something they were unable to do before or do it in a better way.
  • Stakeholders become awesome when they get the value they are looking for in their domain of work.
  • Teams are made awesome when they enjoy their work and feel a sense of accomplishment.
Make safety a priority is the second of the vertical values.
Who needs to be safe? Everyone.
  • Safety is a priority when team members are allowed to make mistakes and correct them.
  • Safety is a priority when customers can rely on the code to not do bad things to them.
  • Safety is a priority when stakeholders know they will be protected from losing value.
Making safety a priority can mean all of these things and more. It involves.
  • Quality
  • Trust
  • Freedom
  • Honesty
  • Diligence
